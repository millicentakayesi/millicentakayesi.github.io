Our Heritage; A research on the Old Kingsway Building, Jamestown

By Millicent Akayesi • 2 mins read
Scroll

Transition of the Old Kingsway Building: Discussing Possibilities for its current use.

I was selected as part of the 2019 cohort of students to participate in an Architectural Research on Heritage Buildings in Jamestown. I researched and documented on the past and present possibilities of the Old Kingsway Building.

North-East view of the Old Kingsway Building, Jamestown

Abandoning historic buildings and leaving them with relatively no use has been a trend from the past to recent times. The Old Kingsway building, however defies this status quo. This building is a valuable place for social activities, a place for community games and interaction, and serves an important function for the people of Ga Mashie.The Old Kingsway Building has been adapted for various uses since its construction to date. Claiming to carry the ‘latest and best’ from London, Kingsway Stores began as a shopping place enabling Europeans to maintain, as fully as possible, their ‘home’ lifestyles in a West African environment (Murillo 2012).

Today, this building serves as a space for all, where the community comes together in times of happiness and in times of sorrow. It has also been beneficial to other people outside the community. It is now an open space bounded by walls, serving multiple purposes for the locals; from outdooring ceremonies to funeral grounds, children’s playground to a place for shooting commercial advertisements and even for hosting boxing events.

The Old Kingsway building has been imbedded within the community and therefore its people should make use of it. (Amartefio 2019). If adaptive reuse is well thought of, there will be more possibilities for this great building whiles it is still being used as an event centre.
